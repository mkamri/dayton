Step Back in Time

Visit Carillon Historical Park, a 65-acre open-air museum with many expansive exhibits on the history of life and innovation in Dayton. The beautiful grounds of Carillon have a lot to offer you and your family, including the Print Shop, Shaker Building, Newcom Tavern (the oldest building in Dayton!), and much more. At the end of your visit, make sure to visit Carillon Brewing Company to enjoy the nation's only fully operational 1850s style brewery and full-service period restaurant!

Early Settlement

Check out this area to feel life in Dayton's roots from the 1800s. Visit the William Morris House to see life from the perspective of a war vet, or stop in to Newcom Tavern to see the area's first jail, church, general store, and courthouse.

The Print Shop

Check out the nation's only fully operational 1930s letterpress job shop. Dayton used to be home to 77 printing companies, including Newsweek, Reader's Digest and Popular Science. Printing 4 million magazines daily, this shop is a testament to the inginuity of Dayton natives.

Carillon Brewing Co.

Visit Carillon Brewing Company for authentic 1850s beer and meals. Taste Dayton's past with a flight of a historical recreations of some of the earliest brews made in Ohio's breweries, or try your hand at brewing with a classic brewer for a day tour!
